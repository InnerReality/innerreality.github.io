Most of the estimates are "normal" in a statistical sense. The mean height of adult humans, the mean production of rice in a given area or compressive strength of concrete are all something that can have a near normal distribution (not true normal cause of lack of negative values in these examples). By central limit theorem, even if the individual sample has a much varied distribution, the mean tends to converge over large number of samples. This is important because of the predictive power at large scales. For example, the production of rice in a significantly large region (say a state) amid constant soil, weather and growth conditions cannot be doubled. The idea behind that local advantages or disadvantages (at a level of farm) are cancelled in a regional scale and thus one can reliably estimate the total production in the given region. However, the normal distribution cannot be used predict the probability of extremes. Within a region, some farms might have produced nothing while some might have produced more than twice the yield. Looking at the distribution (https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2405844022018473) we can say that it is atleast a log-normal distribution (because there is no negative yield). A log-normal distribution is a distribution where the logarithm of a random value follows normal distribution. This leads us to a concept of heavy-tailed distribution.

## Heavy-tails and ?

Heavy tailed distributions are distributions where the tails (that govern extreme probabilitities) decay slower than an exponential distribution (including Normal distribution). This means that extreme events are more likely to occur than when predicted by a normal distribution
